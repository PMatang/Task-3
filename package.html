top destination

Kasol

Kasol, a quaint village in Himachal Pradesh, is nestled in the Parvati Valley along the Parvati River. Known for its breathtaking natural beauty, it is a haven for trekkers and nature enthusiasts. Popular trekking routes like Kheerganga and Malana originate here.

Ladakh

Ladakh, often called the "Land of High Passes," is renowned for its dramatic landscapes featuring rugged mountains, pristine lakes like Pangong Tso and Tso Moriri, and high-altitude deserts. The region offers breathtaking views and unique terrain for adventure seekers.

book now

darjeeling

Darjeeling, nestled in the Himalayan foothills of West Bengal, is famed for its picturesque views of snow-capped peaks, including Kanchenjunga, the world’s third-highest mountain. The lush green tea gardens produce the world-renowned Darjeeling tea.

book now

sikkim

Sikkim, located in the northeastern part of India, is a paradise of lush greenery, alpine meadows, and snow-capped peaks, including Mount Kanchenjunga, the third-highest mountain in the world. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, with places like Yumthang Valley and Khangchendzonga National Park offering stunning landscapes.

book now

jaipur

Jaipur, known as the "Pink City," is a treasure trove of history and architecture. The city is famous for its grand palaces like the City Palace, forts such as Amber and Nahargarh, and iconic landmarks like the Hawa Mahal and Jantar Mantar, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

book now

kutch

Kutch, located in Gujarat, is famed for the mesmerizing Rann of Kutch, a vast salt marsh that transforms into a white desert under the moonlight. The annual Rann Utsav celebrates the region’s culture with music, dance, and local crafts amidst this stunning backdrop.

book now

waynad

Wayanad, a serene district in Kerala, is renowned for its dense forests, misty hills, and tranquil waterfalls like Meenmutty and Soochipara. The Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary is a haven for nature lovers, offering sightings of elephants, tigers, and other wildlife.

book now

meghalaya

Meghalaya, meaning "Abode of Clouds," is a treasure trove of natural beauty. It is home to stunning waterfalls like Nohkalikai and Seven Sisters, as well as lush green valleys and caves like Mawsmai and Liat Prah. The living root bridges of Cherrapunji and Mawlynnong, crafted by local tribes, are iconic landmarks.

book now

tawang

Tawang, located in Arunachal Pradesh, is home to the famous Tawang Monastery, the largest monastery in India and a sacred site for Tibetan Buddhism. Surrounded by stunning landscapes, it serves as a center of spirituality and culture.

book now

dwarka

Dwarka, located in Gujarat, is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and holds immense religious importance in Hinduism. It is believed to be the ancient kingdom of Lord Krishna, with the Dwarkadhish Temple dedicated to him. The city is steeped in mythological stories, especially from the Mahabharata.

book now

junagadh

Junagadh, located in Gujarat, has a rich history reflected in its magnificent forts and palaces. The Junagadh Fort and the Uparkot Fort, with their ancient gates, temples, and step wells, showcase the city’s royal past. The Mahabat Maqbara, a stunning blend of Islamic and Hindu architecture, is also a highlight.

book now

kedarnath

Kedarnath, located in Uttarakhand, is one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The Kedarnath Temple, situated at an altitude of 3,583 meters, is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and attracts thousands of devotees annually, especially during the Kedarnath Yatra.
